# OBSERVATIONS.md Format

`OBSERVATIONS.md` holds the **Critical Observations** — how the topic relates to what Rick already knows. This is the anchor that makes new knowledge stick: new concepts attach to existing ones, or they evaporate. Every deep dive should lead with an observation from here.

## Structure

```md
# {Topic} — Critical Observations

## Similar to
- **{New concept}** is like **{thing Rick already knows}**: {the resemblance that lets him borrow existing intuition}.

## Unique from
- **{New concept}** diverges from **{the thing it resembles}**: {where the analogy breaks — the delta he must not carry over}.

## Connects to
- **{New concept}** plugs into **{tool/workflow Rick already uses}**: {how they compose; what it unlocks in his existing toolkit}.
```

## Rules

- **Lead with what Rick knows.** State the familiar thing first, then the new thing, then the delta. "On Linux you'd… on macOS that fails because… instead you…" is the canonical shape (it's the whole spine of his `macos-cli` course).
- **Similar and Unique travel together.** Every resemblance you record needs its matching divergence — the analogy that isn't corrected is the one that misleads him later.
- **Connections are the payoff.** The point of learning a new tool is to compose it with the toolkit Rick already owns. Record what it plugs into.
- **These are observations, not a glossary.** A tight term definition belongs in `GLOSSARY.md`; a _relationship_ between concepts belongs here.
- **Promote the durable ones.** When an observation corrects a real misconception or unlocks a connection, write a learning record too — those steer future sessions.
